# Horizontal Analysis

<ArticleMeta id={100560} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:51'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p><span style={{backgroundColor:"rgba(0,0,0,0.05)",color:"rgb(36,41,47)"}}>Horizontal Analysis, also known as Trend Analysis, is a method of financial statement analysis that involves comparing financial data across multiple accounting periods to identify trends and changes. This method is typically used to compare financial statement items over several consecutive years to observe their growth or decline trends. For example, one can compare a company's revenue, costs, and profits across different years to assess its financial performance and operational condition. Horizontal Analysis helps to reveal the financial health of a business over different periods and provides valuable insights for decision-makers to formulate more effective strategies and plans.</span></p>
