# EBITDA Interest Coverage Ratio

<ArticleMeta id={102764} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 14:25:55'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>The EBITDA Interest Coverage Ratio is a financial metric used to measure a company's ability to pay interest expenses. It is calculated by dividing EBITDA by interest expenses. A higher ratio indicates that the company is more capable of covering its interest payments, reflecting a stronger financial position.</p>
