# Contra Account

<ArticleMeta id={100369} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A contra account is an account used to reduce or offset the balance of another related account. It is typically used in accounting to decrease or neutralize the original balance of assets, liabilities, revenues, or expenses. The balance of a contra account is usually opposite to that of the associated account. For instance, a contra account for an asset account will usually have a credit balance, while a contra account for a liability account will have a debit balance. Common examples of contra accounts include Accumulated Depreciation (used to offset the book value of fixed assets) and Allowance for Doubtful Accounts (used to offset the book value of accounts receivable).</p>
