# Accrued Expenses

<ArticleMeta id={289} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 20:56:36'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

Accrued expenses refer to expenses that have been incurred by a company during the current accounting period but have not yet been paid. Accrued expenses are included in the financial statements as part of liabilities or assets to reflect the company's actual financial condition.

<AIContent content={`<p><strong>Definition:</strong> Accrued expenses refer to expenses that have been incurred by a business during the current accounting period but have not yet been paid. These expenses are listed as part of liabilities or assets in the financial statements to reflect the actual financial condition of the business. Common accrued expenses include wages, interest, and rent.</p><p><strong>Origin:</strong> The concept of accrued expenses originates from the accrual accounting principle, which requires businesses to record expenses when they are incurred, not when they are paid. This concept helps businesses more accurately reflect their financial condition and operating results.</p><p><strong>Categories and Characteristics:</strong> Accrued expenses can be divided into several types, mainly including:<ul><li><strong>Accrued Wages:</strong> Refers to employee wages that have been incurred but not yet paid during the accounting period.</li><li><strong>Accrued Interest:</strong> Refers to loan interest that has been incurred but not yet paid during the accounting period.</li><li><strong>Accrued Rent:</strong> Refers to rent expenses that have been incurred but not yet paid during the accounting period.</li></ul>These accrued expenses share the common characteristic of being recorded when the expense is incurred, not when it is paid.</p><p><strong>Specific Cases:</strong><ul><li><strong>Case 1:</strong> A company accrued employee wages of 100,000 yuan on December 31, although the actual payment was made in January of the following year. This accrued expense is listed as a liability in the financial statements as of December 31, reflecting the company's actual expenses for the period.</li><li><strong>Case 2:</strong> A business accrued loan interest of 50,000 yuan on December 31, with the actual payment made in January of the following year.
