# Cash Flow from Operating Activities 

<ArticleMeta id={102544} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:59:02'} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

Cash flow from operating activities (CFO) indicates the amount of money a company brings in from its ongoing, regular business activities, such as manufacturing and selling goods or providing a service to customers. It is the first section depicted on a company's cash flow statement.Cash flow from operating activities does not include long-term capital expenditures or investment revenue and expense.
