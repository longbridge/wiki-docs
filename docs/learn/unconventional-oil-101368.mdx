# Unconventional Oil

<ArticleMeta id={101368} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:54'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Unconventional oil refers to petroleum resources that cannot be easily extracted using traditional drilling and pumping techniques. These resources typically include oil sands, shale oil, heavy oil, and ultra-heavy oil. The extraction and production of unconventional oil require specialized technologies and methods, such as hydraulic fracturing (fracking), horizontal drilling, and thermal recovery techniques. Due to the complexity and difficulty of extraction, the production costs of unconventional oil are generally higher than those of conventional oil. However, with advancements in technology and fluctuations in oil prices, unconventional oil has become increasingly significant in the global energy supply. The development of unconventional oil also poses environmental challenges, including water usage, geological disturbances, and carbon emissions.</p>
