# Middle-Income Countries 

<ArticleMeta id={102234} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 17:18:06'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Middle-Income Countries (MICs) are those nations with a per capita gross national income (GNI) that falls between the levels of low-income countries and high-income countries. The World Bank classifies countries based on per capita GNI into low-income, middle-income, and high-income categories, with middle-income countries further divided into lower-middle-income and upper-middle-income countries.</p><p>The World Bank's specific classification criteria are:</p><p>Lower-Middle-Income Countries: Per capita GNI between $1,046 and $4,095.<br/>Upper-Middle-Income Countries: Per capita GNI between $4,096 and $12,695.<br/>Characteristics of middle-income countries include:</p><p>Economic Diversity: Diverse economic structures that include agriculture, industry, and services.<br/>Development Potential: Typically have high development potential and rapid economic growth rates, but also face challenges such as development imbalances and structural issues.<br/>Social Development: Social indicators such as education, healthcare, and infrastructure are generally better than those in low-income countries but still lag behind those in high-income countries.<br/>International Status: Have a certain level of influence and voice in international affairs, often acting as regional economic and political powers.<br/>Middle-income countries play a crucial role in the global economy and have significant potential for growth and development.</p>
