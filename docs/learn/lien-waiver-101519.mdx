# Lien Waiver

<ArticleMeta id={101519} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:58'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

A lien waiver is a written agreement between a payer and a counterparty where said counterparty gives up their right to place a lien on the payer's property or goods.

<AIContent content={`<p><strong>Definition:</strong> Waiver of lien refers to a written agreement between the payer and the counterparty, where the counterparty waives the right to establish a lien on the payer's property or goods. The modern legal framework for liens was gradually perfected in the 19th century, especially in commercial and financial transactions. Waiver of lien, as a contractual clause, aims to protect the payer's interests and prevent their property from being retained.</p><p><strong>Categories and Characteristics:</strong> Waiver of lien can be divided into two categories:<ul><li><strong>Full Waiver:</strong> The counterparty completely waives the right to establish a lien on the payer's property under any circumstances. This type of waiver is often used in long-term cooperative relationships to build trust.</li><li><strong>Limited Waiver:</strong> The counterparty waives the lien under specific conditions, such as for a particular transaction or within a specific time frame. This type of waiver is more flexible and suitable for short-term or one-time transactions.</li></ul>The main characteristics of waiver of lien include:<ul><li>Protecting the payer's property from being retained.</li><li>Enhancing transparency and trust in transactions.</li><li>May require additional fees or other forms of collateral.</li></ul></p><p><strong>Specific Cases:</strong><ul><li><strong>Case 1:</strong> A manufacturing company signs a contract with its supplier, where the supplier agrees to waive the right to establish a lien on the raw materials stored in the manufacturing company's warehouse.
