# Unbiased Predictor

<ArticleMeta id={101662} updatedAt={'2024-09-13 10:31:09'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

Expectations theory attempts to predict what short-term interest rates will be in the future based on current long-term interest rates. The theory suggests that an investor earns the same interest by investing in two consecutive one-year bond investments versus investing in one two-year bond today. The theory is also known as the "unbiased expectations theory."

The characteristics of the Unbiased Predictor are based on market efficiency and rational expectations, assuming that investors can make accurate predictions using market information.</p><p><strong>Specific Cases:</strong> 1. <em>Case One:</em> Suppose the current one-year bond interest rate is 2%, and the market expects the one-year bond interest rate to be 3% next year.
