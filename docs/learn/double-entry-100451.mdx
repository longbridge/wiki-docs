# Double-entry Bookkeeping

<ArticleMeta id={100451} updatedAt={'2024-09-13 11:33:10'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Double-entry bookkeeping is an accounting method where each transaction is recorded in at least two accounts to ensure the accounting equation (Assets = Liabilities + Equity) remains balanced. This method was first introduced by the Italian mathematician Luca Pacioli in the 15th century and forms the foundation of modern accounting.</p>
