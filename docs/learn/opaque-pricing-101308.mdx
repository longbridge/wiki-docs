# Opaque Pricing

<ArticleMeta id={101308} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:54'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Opaque pricing refers to a pricing strategy or market condition where the price information of goods or services is not fully disclosed or transparent to consumers or market participants. In such cases, the buyer may not know the exact price or pricing mechanism in advance and often only learns the final price upon completing the purchase. Opaque pricing is common in industries like travel (e.g., airline tickets, hotel bookings) and auctions. This strategy is sometimes used to increase sales volume, optimize inventory, or maximize profits, but it can also lead to consumer dissatisfaction and a decrease in trust towards the business.</p>
