# Candlestick Chart

<ArticleMeta id={100131} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 20:49:07'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A candlestick is a type of price chart used in technical analysis. It displays the high, low, open, and closing prices of a security for a specific period. The candlestick originated from Japanese rice merchants and traders hundreds of years before becoming popularized in the United States. It was used to track market prices and daily momentum.</p><p>The wide part of the candlestick is called the "<a href="https://www.investopedia.com/terms/r/realbody.asp"><u>real body</u></a>." It tells investors whether the closing price is higher or lower than the opening price. It appears as black/red if the stock closed lower or white/green if the stock closed higher.</p>
