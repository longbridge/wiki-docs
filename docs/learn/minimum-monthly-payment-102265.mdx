# Minimum Monthly Payment

<ArticleMeta id={102265} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:59:00'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>The Minimum Monthly Payment refers to the smallest amount that a borrower is required to pay each month to keep their account in good standing and avoid penalties or default. This amount is typically associated with credit card bills and loan repayment plans. The minimum monthly payment usually includes the interest due for the month, a portion of the principal, and any applicable fees or penalties. Paying the minimum monthly payment can prevent the account from becoming overdue, but it usually does not significantly reduce the total owed because most of the payment covers interest and fees.</p><p>For credit cards:</p><ol><li><strong>Interest Payment</strong>: The minimum monthly payment includes the interest accrued for the month.</li><li><strong>Partial Principal</strong>: A small portion of the minimum monthly payment goes toward repaying the principal.</li><li><strong>Fees and Penalties</strong>: Any late fees or other charges are also included in the minimum monthly payment.</li></ol><p>While making the minimum monthly payment keeps the account in good standing, consistently paying only the minimum amount can increase the total repayment amount and extend the repayment period. Therefore, it is a prudent financial strategy to pay off as much of the debt as possible each month.</p>
