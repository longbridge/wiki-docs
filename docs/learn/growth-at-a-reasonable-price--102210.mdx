# Growth At A Reasonable Price 

<ArticleMeta id={102210} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 17:25:35'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Growth At A Reasonable Price (GARP) is an investment strategy that combines the principles of value investing and growth investing. The aim is to find stocks that have strong growth potential but are reasonably priced, thereby balancing the risks and capturing the benefits of price appreciation.</p><p>Key characteristics of GARP include:</p><p>Growth Potential: Focus on companies with high earnings growth potential.<br/>Reasonable Valuation: Avoid overvalued stocks by seeking those with lower price-to-earnings (P/E) ratios or price/earnings to growth (PEG) ratios.<br/>Risk-Reward Balance: Reduce investment risk and enhance long-term returns by balancing growth and valuation.<br/>Comprehensive Analysis: Utilize fundamental, technical, and quantitative analysis to comprehensively evaluate stocks.<br/>Core metrics of the GARP strategy:</p><p>Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio: Assesses the stock price relative to its earnings per share. A lower P/E ratio typically indicates a more reasonably valued stock.<br/>Price/Earnings to Growth (PEG) Ratio: Combines the P/E ratio with the company's earnings growth rate. The formula is:<br/>PEG = (P/E)/Expected&nbsp;Earnings&nbsp;Growth&nbsp;Rate<br/><br/>A lower PEG ratio (typically less than 1) suggests the stock has good growth potential and reasonable valuation.</p>
