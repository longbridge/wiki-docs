# Loan Lock

<ArticleMeta id={100354} updatedAt={'2024-09-13 11:38:02'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A loan lock, also known as a rate lock, is an agreement between a borrower and a lender that secures a specific interest rate on a loan for a predetermined period, known as the lock-in period. This period can be 30 days, 45 days, 60 days, or longer. The purpose of a loan lock is to protect the borrower from fluctuations in market interest rates. During the lock-in period, even if market rates rise, the borrower is guaranteed the lower rate that was locked in. Loan locks are commonly used in the mortgage application process to ensure that the borrower won't face increased costs due to rate changes during the loan approval and home closing period.</p>
