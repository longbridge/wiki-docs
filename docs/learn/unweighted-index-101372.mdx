# Unweighted Index

<ArticleMeta id={101372} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:54'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>An unweighted index is a type of stock market index that is calculated by simply averaging the prices of all the constituent stocks without considering their market capitalization or trading volume. This means that each stock in the index has the same weight, regardless of the company's size or market performance. A typical example of an unweighted index is the Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA), although it is technically price-weighted, its calculation method is closer to the concept of an unweighted index.</p>
