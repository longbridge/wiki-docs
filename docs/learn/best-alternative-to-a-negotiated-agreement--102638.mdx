# Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement 

<ArticleMeta id={102638} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 00:23:46'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A best alternative to a negotiated agreement (BATNA) is a course of action that a party engaged in negotiations has determined should be taken if talks fail and no agreement can be reached.</p><p>Negotiation researchers Roger Fisher and William Ury coined the term BATNA in their 1981 bestseller, "Getting to Yes: Negotiating Agreement Without Giving In."</p><p>A party's BATNA is the position it will fall back on if negotiation proves unsuccessful.</p>

The book emphasizes the importance of understanding and developing one's BATNA to maintain an advantage in negotiations.</p><p><strong>Categories and Characteristics:</strong> BATNA can be categorized based on the context and goals of the negotiation.
