# Hybrid Adjustable-Rate Mortgage

<ArticleMeta id={102659} updatedAt={'2024-09-11 23:57:09'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A hybrid adjustable-rate mortgage blends characteristics of a fixed-rate mortgage with an adjustable-rate mortgage. A hybrid ARM will have an initial fixed interest rate period followed by an adjustable rate period. After the fixed rate expires, the rate adjusts based on an index plus a margin. The date at which the mortgage changes from the fixed rate to the adjustable rate is referred to as the reset date.</p><p>The most common configuration of hybrid ARM is the 5/1, which has an initial fixed term of 5 years followed by adjustable rates that reset every 12 months.</p>
