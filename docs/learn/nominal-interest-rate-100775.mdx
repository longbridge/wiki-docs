# Nominal Interest Rate

<ArticleMeta id={100775} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:51'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

Nominal interest rate refers to the interest rate before taking inflation into account. Nominal can also refer to the advertised or stated interest rate on a loan, without taking into account any fees or compounding of interest.

Fixed nominal interest rates remain constant throughout the loan or investment period, suitable for stable economic environments; floating nominal interest rates adjust according to changes in market rates, suitable for environments with significant interest rate fluctuations. The main characteristic of nominal interest rates is their simplicity, but they do not reflect changes in actual purchasing power.</p><p><strong>Specific Cases:</strong> Case 1: A bank offers a loan with a fixed annual interest rate of 5%, which is the nominal interest rate.
