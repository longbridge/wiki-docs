# Premiums Receivable

<ArticleMeta id={117} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 21:05:53'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Premiums Receivable refers to the amounts an insurance company is owed for insurance premiums that have been billed but not yet paid by policyholders. These amounts are recorded as receivables on the company's financial statements and represent future cash inflows.</p>

<AIContent content={`<p><strong>Definition:</strong> Accounts receivable premiums refer to the insurance fees that an insurance company has not yet received after the insurance contract has taken effect. These unpaid insurance fees are known as accounts receivable premiums.</p><p><strong>Origin:</strong> The concept of accounts receivable premiums emerged with the development of the insurance industry. Early insurance contracts typically required the insured to pay the full premium before the contract took effect.
