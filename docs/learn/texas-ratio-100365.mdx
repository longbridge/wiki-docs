# Texas Ratio

<ArticleMeta id={100365} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 20:37:27'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>The Texas Ratio is a financial metric used to evaluate the health of a bank or financial institution. It measures the level of risk by comparing the institution's non-performing assets (including non-performing loans and foreclosed properties) to its tangible equity capital and loan loss reserves. The formula is as follows:</p><p>Texas&nbsp;Ratio= (Non-performing&nbsp;Loans+Owned&nbsp;Real&nbsp;Estate)/（Tangible&nbsp;Equity&nbsp;Capital+Loan&nbsp;Loss&nbsp;Reserves）.&nbsp;</p><p>A higher Texas Ratio indicates greater financial stress and a higher likelihood of potential failure. Generally, if the Texas Ratio exceeds 100%, the bank may be facing significant financial difficulties.</p>
