# Liquidity Coverage Ratio 

<ArticleMeta id={102032} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 03:46:18'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>The Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR) is a key regulatory metric for measuring the liquidity position of financial institutions, determined by calculating the ratio of high-quality liquid assets held by the institution to its projected net cash outflows over a 30-day period. The purpose of the LCR is to ensure that financial institutions maintain sufficient liquidity to meet their short-term obligations even under market stress.</p><p>The Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR) is part of Basel III and aims to improve banks' resilience to liquidity risks. Through LCR regulatory requirements, regulators encourage financial institutions to hold a certain proportion of high-quality liquid assets to deal with potential market liquidity risks. &nbsp; &nbsp;</p>
