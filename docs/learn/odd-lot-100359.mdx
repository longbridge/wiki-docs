# Odd Lot

<ArticleMeta id={100359} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 20:37:42'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Odd lot refers to the trading of stock in quantities that are less than the standard trading unit, typically 100 shares. In the stock market, a standard trading unit is often called a "round lot," and trades that involve fewer shares than a round lot are called odd lot trades. Odd lot trading is usually conducted by small investors who may not have sufficient funds to purchase a full round lot of shares. The transaction fees for odd lot trades can be higher, and in some markets, odd lot trades may have lower liquidity.</p>
