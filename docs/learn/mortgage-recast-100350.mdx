# Mortgage Recast

<ArticleMeta id={100350} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A mortgage recast refers to the process where a borrower makes a large lump-sum payment towards the principal balance of an existing mortgage, leading to a recalculation of the monthly payments for the remaining loan term. Typically, a recast does not alter the loan's term or interest rate but instead recalculates the monthly payments based on the new, lower principal balance and the remaining loan term. This can help borrowers reduce their monthly payment burden without the need for a full refinance. Mortgage recast is usually available for fixed-rate mortgages, but the specific terms and conditions can vary by lender.</p>
