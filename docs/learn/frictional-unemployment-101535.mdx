# Frictional Unemployment

<ArticleMeta id={101535} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:58'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Frictional unemployment is a type of short-term unemployment that occurs when workers look for new employment or transition out of old jobs and into new ones. This temporary period of unemployment is the result of voluntary transitions within an economy. It stands in contrast with structural unemployment, which stems from economic shifts that make it difficult for workers to find work.</p><p>Frictional unemployment can be evident in a growing, stable economy and is regarded as a part of natural unemployment, the minimum&nbsp;unemployment&nbsp;rate in an economy due to economic forces and the movement of labor.</p><p>The frictional unemployment rate is calculated by dividing the workers actively looking for jobs by the total labor force. The workers actively looking for jobs are typically classified into three categories: workers who left their job, people returning to the workforce, and new entrants.</p>
