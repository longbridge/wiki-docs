# Interest-Only Mortgage

<ArticleMeta id={100236} updatedAt={'2024-09-13 11:44:44'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>An Interest-Only Mortgage is a type of mortgage where, for a set period of time at the beginning of the loan term, the borrower is required to pay only the interest on the loan, not the principal. This interest-only period typically lasts for 5 to 10 years. After this period, the borrower must begin paying both the principal and the interest, resulting in higher monthly payments.</p><p>The advantage of an interest-only mortgage is that the initial monthly payments are lower, which can help borrowers manage their finances more easily in the early years of home ownership. However, the downside is that the principal balance of the loan does not decrease during the interest-only period, potentially leading to significantly higher payments later on. Additionally, if the property value declines, the borrower may owe more than the property is worth.</p>
