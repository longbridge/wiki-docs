# Synthetic Options

<ArticleMeta id={306} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 20:55:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Synthetic options are a type of composite derivative created by combining multiple financial instruments (such as stocks, options, futures) to mimic the price or risk characteristics of an underlying asset. Synthetic options are primarily used to operate in markets where direct trading of the underlying asset is not available. By combining various financial instruments, synthetic options replicate the returns and risk profiles of the underlying asset.</p>
