# Junior Equity

<ArticleMeta id={100331} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Junior Equity refers to the lowest priority equity in a company's capital structure. In the event of liquidation or bankruptcy, junior equity is repaid after all debts and preferred equity. Junior equity typically refers to common stock, which represents residual ownership in the company. Because junior equity is last in line for repayment, holders face higher risk but also have the potential for higher returns. When the company is profitable, junior equity holders can benefit from dividends and stock price appreciation, but they may incur losses if the company's performance is poor.</p>
