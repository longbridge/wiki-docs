# Revocable Trust

<ArticleMeta id={100304} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A revocable trust is a trust arrangement that allows the grantor to modify, revoke, or terminate the trust during their lifetime. The grantor typically transfers assets into the trust and may appoint themselves or another person as the trustee to manage these assets. One of the main advantages of a revocable trust is its flexibility, allowing the grantor to adjust the terms of the trust as needed. Additionally, a revocable trust can help avoid probate (the court process of validating a will), thereby speeding up the distribution of assets and maintaining privacy. However, unlike irrevocable trusts, the assets in a revocable trust are still considered part of the grantor's estate upon their death and may be subject to estate taxes.</p>
