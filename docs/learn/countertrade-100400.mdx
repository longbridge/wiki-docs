# Counter Trade

<ArticleMeta id={100400} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Counter Trade is a form of international trade where goods or services are exchanged instead of using cash. This type of trade is often conducted between countries facing currency shortages or strict foreign exchange controls to facilitate international trade and economic cooperation.</p><p>Main forms of counter trade include:</p><ol><li>Barter: The simplest form of counter trade where goods or services are directly exchanged without the use of money.&nbsp;</li><li>Counterpurchase: One party agrees to purchase goods or services from another party on the condition that the latter purchases an equivalent value of goods or services from the former at a future date.&nbsp;</li><li>Buyback: The exporter provides production equipment or technology with the condition that the importer will pay for it with products produced using that equipment or technology in the future.&nbsp;</li><li>Compensation Trade: A combination of part cash and part goods or services.</li></ol>
