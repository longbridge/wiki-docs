# Chart Of Accounts 

<ArticleMeta id={101439} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:54'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A Chart of Accounts (COA) is a comprehensive list of all accounts used by an organization to record financial transactions. Each account has a unique number and name, facilitating financial reporting and analysis. Main categories include:</p><ul><li><strong>Assets</strong>: e.g., cash, accounts receivable, inventory.</li><li><strong>Liabilities</strong>: e.g., accounts payable, short-term loans.</li><li><strong>Equity</strong>: e.g., common stock, retained earnings.</li><li><strong>Revenues</strong>: e.g., sales revenue, service revenue.</li><li><strong>Expenses</strong>: e.g., salaries, rent.</li></ul>
