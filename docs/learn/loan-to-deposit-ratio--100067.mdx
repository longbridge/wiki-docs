# Loan-To-Deposit Ratio

<ArticleMeta id={100067} updatedAt={'2024-09-13 11:51:45'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>The loan-to-deposit ratio (LDR) is used to assess&nbsp;a bank's liquidity by comparing&nbsp;a bank's total loans to its total deposits for the same period. The LDR is expressed as a percentage. If the ratio is too high, it means that the bank may not have enough liquidity to cover any unforeseen fund requirements. Conversely, if the ratio is too low, the bank may not be earning as much as it could be.</p><p>Formula:</p><figure class="image image_resized" style={{width:"47.23%"}}><img src="https://pub.lbkrs.com/social/2024/0/Nokji3HWNAKKZKe81BXFqp3ApCt99jz9.jpg" /></figure>
