# Interest Expense

<p><span style={{backgroundColor:"rgba(0,0,0,0.05)",color:"rgb(36,41,47)"}}>Interest Expense refers to the cost incurred by a company for borrowed funds during a specific period. These borrowed funds can include bank loans, bonds, commercial paper, etc. Interest expense is typically listed on the company's income statement as part of financial expenses. It reflects the cost that the company pays for raising funds and directly impacts the net profit and cash flow. Interest expense is part of the operational costs of a business, and effective debt management can help optimize the capital structure and reduce financing costs.</span></p>
