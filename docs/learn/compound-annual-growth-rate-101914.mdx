# Compound Annual Growth Rate

<ArticleMeta id={101914} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 18:57:45'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>The compound annual growth rate (CAGR) is the rate of return (RoR) that would be required for an investment to grow from its beginning balance to its ending balance, assuming the profits were reinvested at the end of each period of the investment’s life span.</p><p>CAGR provides a concise way to assess the overall performance of an investment over a period of time, regardless of fluctuations over time. Although CAGR is a useful metric, it may not fully reflect the actual compounding effect over the period, especially when the ROI fluctuates greatly. &nbsp;</p>

Suitable for investments with regular cash flows, such as dividend-paying stocks or bonds.</p><p>3. **Real CAGR**: Considers factors like inflation, reflecting the actual growth in purchasing power of the investment.</p><h3>Specific Cases</h3><p>Case 1: Suppose you invested $10,000 at the beginning of 2019, and by the beginning of 2024, your investment grew to $15,000.
