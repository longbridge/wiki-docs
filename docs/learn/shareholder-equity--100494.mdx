# Shareholder Equity 

<ArticleMeta id={100494} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Shareholder equity (also known as equity capital, owners' equity, or stockholders' equity) represents the residual interest in the assets of a company after deducting liabilities. It includes paid-in capital, additional paid-in capital, retained earnings, and other comprehensive income. Shareholder equity reflects the financial obligations of a company to its shareholders and is a key indicator of the company's financial health. An increase in shareholder equity typically indicates strong profitability and good financial condition of the company.</p>
