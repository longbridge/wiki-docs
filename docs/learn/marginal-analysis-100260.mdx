# Marginal Analysis

<ArticleMeta id={100260} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Marginal analysis is an economic decision-making tool that involves evaluating the additional benefits (marginal benefits) and additional costs (marginal costs) of a one-unit change in an activity, such as production, consumption, or investment. By comparing marginal benefits and marginal costs, firms and individuals can make optimal decisions to maximize profit or utility. The key principle in marginal analysis is that if the marginal benefit exceeds the marginal cost, increasing the activity is advantageous; if the marginal benefit is less than the marginal cost, decreasing the activity is advantageous. Marginal analysis is widely used in microeconomics to help explain and predict the behavior of firms and consumers.</p>
