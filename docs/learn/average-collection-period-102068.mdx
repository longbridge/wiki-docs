# Average Collection Period

<ArticleMeta id={102068} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 03:37:50'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>The Average Collection Period (ACP) refers to the average number of days it takes for a company to receive payment from its customers after a sale has been made. This metric reflects the efficiency of a company's accounts receivable management and the timeliness of customer payments. A shorter ACP indicates that a company can quickly collect cash, improving its cash flow and operational efficiency; a longer ACP may suggest issues in accounts receivable management or customer credit control.</p><p>Key characteristics include:</p><ol><li><strong>Collection Efficiency</strong>: Measures the efficiency of a company's accounts receivable collection and the timeliness of customer payments.</li><li><strong>Financial Management Metric</strong>: Used to evaluate a company's cash flow and financial management effectiveness.</li><li><strong>Credit Control</strong>: Reflects the effectiveness of the company's credit policies and customer management.</li><li><strong>Cash Flow Relationship</strong>: A shorter ACP indicates more stable cash flow, while a longer ACP may increase cash flow pressure.</li></ol><p>The formula for calculating the Average Collection Period:&nbsp;</p><p>Average&nbsp;Collection&nbsp;Period = (Accounts&nbsp;Receivable/Annual&nbsp;Sales)×365&nbsp;</p><p>or&nbsp;</p><p>Average&nbsp;Collection&nbsp;Period = Accounts&nbsp;Receivable/Daily&nbsp;Sales&nbsp;</p><p>Example application: Suppose a company has an accounts receivable balance of $100,000 at the end of the year and annual sales of $1,000,000. The Average Collection Period would be calculated as follows:&nbsp;</p><p>Average&nbsp;Collection&nbsp;Period = (100,000/1,000,000)×365 = 36.5&nbsp;days</p><p>This means it takes the company an average of 36.5 days to collect payment after making a sale.</p>
