# Accounting Ratio

<ArticleMeta id={100581} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:51'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>An Accounting Ratio, also known as a Financial Ratio, is a ratio derived from data in financial statements used to analyze and assess a company's financial condition, operational efficiency, and profitability. Common accounting ratios include the current ratio, quick ratio, debt-to-equity ratio, gross profit margin, net profit margin, and return on assets. These ratios help investors, management, and other stakeholders understand the financial health and performance of a business. For example, the current ratio measures a company's short-term liquidity, while the net profit margin indicates its profitability. Accounting ratios are essential tools in financial analysis, aiding in making more informed investment and management decisions.</p>
