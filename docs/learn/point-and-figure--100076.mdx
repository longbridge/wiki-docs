# Point-And-Figure Chart

<ArticleMeta id={100076} updatedAt={'2024-09-13 11:51:29'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A point-and-figure chart plots price movements for stocks, bonds, commodities, or futures without taking into consideration the passage of time.</p><p>Contrary to some other types of charts, like candlesticks, which mark the degree of an asset's movement over set time periods, P&amp;F charts utilize columns consisting of stacked X's or O's, each of which represents a set amount of price movement. The X's illustrate rising prices, while O's represent a falling price.</p><p>Technical analysts still utilize concepts such as support and resistance, as well as other patterns, when viewing P&amp;F charts. Some argue that support and resistance levels, as well as breakouts, are more clearly defined on a P&amp;F chart since it filters out tiny price movements and is less susceptible to false breakouts.</p>
