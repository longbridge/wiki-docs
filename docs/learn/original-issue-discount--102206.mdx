# Original Issue Discount 

<ArticleMeta id={102206} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 17:27:48'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Original Issue Discount (OID) refers to the situation where a bond or other debt instrument is issued at a price lower than its face value. The OID represents the additional income that investors will receive when the bond matures, equal to the difference between the bond's face value and its issue price. This additional income is typically treated as interest income and is gradually included in the investor's taxable income over the life of the bond.</p><p>Key characteristics of Original Issue Discount include:</p><p>Issued Below Face Value: The bond is issued at a price lower than its face value, providing investors with an immediate discount at purchase.<br/>Tax Treatment: OID is considered interest income and must be gradually taxed over the bond's holding period rather than being taxed in a lump sum at maturity.<br/>Increased Yield: Through OID, investors can receive the difference between the face value and the issue price as additional income upon bond maturity.<br/>Pricing Strategy: Issuers can use OID to attract more investors, especially when market interest rates are high, making the bond more appealing.<br/>Example of calculating Original Issue Discount:<br/>Suppose a company issues a bond with a face value of $1,000 but sells it for $950, with a maturity period of 5 years. The OID for this bond is:<br/>OID = Face&nbsp;Value−Issue&nbsp;Price = 1000−950 = 50USD</p><p>For tax purposes, assuming this $50 OID must be reported as taxable income over the 5-year holding period, the annual taxable OID income would be:<br/>50 USD/5 years = 10USD/year</p>
