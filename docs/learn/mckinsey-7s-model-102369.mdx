# Mckinsey 7S Model

<ArticleMeta id={102369} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 16:35:25'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

The McKinsey 7S Model is a framework for organizational effectiveness that postulates that there are seven internal factors of an organization that need to be aligned and reinforced in order for it to be successful.

The model was initially designed to help companies maintain consistency and coordination during periods of change.</p><h3>Categories and Characteristics</h3><p>1. <strong>Strategy</strong>: The plan and actions an organization takes to achieve its goals.</p><p>2. <strong>Structure</strong>: The organizational architecture and hierarchy.</p><p>3. <strong>Systems</strong>: The processes and procedures within the organization.</p><p>4. <strong>Shared Values</strong>: The organizational culture and core values.</p><p>5. <strong>Skills</strong>: The capabilities and expertise of the organization's members.</p><p>6. <strong>Staff</strong>: The employees and human resources of the organization.</p><p>7. <strong>Style</strong>: The leadership style and management approach.</p><h3>Specific Cases</h3><p>Case 1: A tech company experiencing rapid expansion found that internal communication issues were causing project delays.
