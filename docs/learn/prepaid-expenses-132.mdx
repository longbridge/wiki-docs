# Prepaid Expenses

<ArticleMeta id={132} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 21:05:02'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Prepaid Expenses refer to expenses that have been paid in advance but have not yet been recognized as expenses in the current accounting period. These expenses will be amortized over future accounting periods as they are incurred.</p>

<AIContent content={`<p><strong>Definition:</strong> Deferred expenses refer to costs that a company has already paid but has not yet recognized as expenses in the current period. These expenses will be gradually amortized over future accounting periods and recognized as current expenses. Common deferred expenses include prepaid rent and prepaid insurance.</p><p><strong>Origin:</strong> The concept of deferred expenses originates from the accrual accounting principle. The accrual principle requires companies to recognize expenses when they are incurred, not when cash is paid. This principle ensures the accuracy and consistency of financial statements.</p><p><strong>Categories and Characteristics:</strong> Deferred expenses can be classified into short-term and long-term deferred expenses. Short-term deferred expenses are typically amortized within one year, such as quarterly prepaid rent; long-term deferred expenses are amortized over periods longer than one year, such as multi-year prepaid insurance. The characteristics of deferred expenses are that they have been paid but not yet recognized and need to be gradually amortized over future accounting periods.</p><p><strong>Specific Cases:</strong> Case 1: A company pays an annual rent of 120,000 yuan on January 1, 2024. According to the accrual principle, the company needs to amortize 10,000 yuan of rent expense each month. Case 2: A company pays a three-year insurance premium of 36,000 yuan on January 1, 2024. It amortizes 12,000 yuan each year. In the financial statement for 2024, only 12,000 yuan is recognized as the current expense, while the remaining 24,000 yuan is recorded as deferred expense.</p><p><strong>Common Questions:</strong> 1. What is the difference between deferred expenses and prepaid expenses?
