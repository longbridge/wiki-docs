# Marginal Utility

<ArticleMeta id={100263} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Marginal utility refers to the additional satisfaction or happiness a consumer gains from consuming one more unit of a good or service. This concept is fundamental in economics and is used to explain consumer behavior and the shape of demand curves. Typically, as the quantity consumed increases, the marginal utility decreases, a phenomenon known as the law of diminishing marginal utility. Marginal utility plays a crucial role in decision-making processes, helping consumers and businesses make optimal choices given limited resources.</p>
