# Horizontal Market

<ArticleMeta id={100564} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:51'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p><span style={{backgroundColor:"rgba(0,0,0,0.05)",color:"rgb(36,41,47)"}}>A Horizontal Market refers to a type of market where a product or service can meet the needs of multiple industries or sectors, rather than being confined to a specific industry. Products or services in a horizontal market have a wide range of applications and are suitable for users across various industries. Examples of horizontal markets include computer software, office equipment, and internet services, as these products and services can be used by companies in different sectors. The advantage of a horizontal market lies in its broad customer base and diverse application scenarios, which help reduce market risk and enhance business stability. Companies competing in a horizontal market typically focus on the versatility and flexibility of their products to meet the needs of different customers.</span></p>
