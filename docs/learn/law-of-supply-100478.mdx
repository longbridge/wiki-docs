# Law Of Supply

<ArticleMeta id={100478} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>The Law of Supply states that, all else being equal, an increase in the price of a good or service will result in an increase in the quantity supplied, and a decrease in the price will result in a decrease in the quantity supplied. This principle reflects producers' response to price signals, where higher prices typically lead to higher profits, thereby incentivizing producers to supply more.</p>

Price changes may not immediately lead to significant changes in supply quantity.</li><li><strong>Long-term supply:</strong> In the long term, producers can adjust production scale, technology, and resource allocation, so the elasticity of supply is high. Price changes will lead to significant changes in supply quantity.</li></ul></p><p><strong>Specific Cases:</strong><ul><li><strong>Case 1:</strong> Suppose the market price of an agricultural product rises.
