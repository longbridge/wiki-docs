This method can lead to the neglect of base rates.</li><li><strong>Availability Heuristic:</strong> People assess the frequency or probability of an event based on the ease with which examples come to mind. This can result in overestimating the importance of recent or vivid events.</li><li><strong>Anchoring Heuristic:</strong> People rely heavily on the first piece of information (the anchor) when making decisions, even if it is irrelevant.</li></ul><h3>Specific Cases</h3><p><strong>Case One:</strong> In investment decisions, investors might use the availability heuristic to judge the risk of a particular stock.
