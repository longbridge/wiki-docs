# Marginal Profit

<ArticleMeta id={100261} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Marginal profit refers to the additional profit that a firm earns from selling one more unit of a product or service. It is calculated as the difference between marginal revenue and marginal cost. Marginal profit plays a crucial role in a firm's production and pricing decisions, as it helps determine the optimal level of production to maximize profit. When marginal profit is positive, increasing production will increase total profit; when marginal profit is negative, increasing production will decrease total profit. Therefore, firms typically stop increasing production when marginal profit is zero, as this is the point where profit is maximized.</p>
