# Net Debt Per Capita

<ArticleMeta id={101585} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:58'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

Net debt per capita is a measurement of the value of a government's debt expressed in terms of the amount attributable to each citizen under the government's jurisdiction.

As government debt issues became more severe, this indicator gradually gained widespread use in the late 20th century.</p><p><strong>Categories and Characteristics:</strong> Per capita net debt can be divided into the following categories:<ul><li><strong>Nominal per capita net debt:</strong> Calculated using current debt and population data without considering inflation factors.</li><li><strong>Real per capita net debt:</strong> Calculated using adjusted debt and population data considering inflation factors.</li></ul>Characteristics:<ul><li>Intuitiveness: By using per capita data, ordinary citizens can more easily understand the scale of government debt.</li><li>Comparability: Facilitates comparison of debt levels between different countries or regions.</li><li>Dynamic nature: Per capita net debt changes with variations in population and debt.</li></ul></p><p><strong>Specific Cases:</strong><ul><li><strong>Case 1:</strong> Suppose a country has a total debt of $1 trillion, total assets of $200 billion, and a total population of 300 million.
