# Ceded premiums

<ArticleMeta id={59} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 21:08:39'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Ceded premiums refers to the premiums obtained by insurance companies for assuming insurance risks during a specified period as stipulated in the insurance contract. This portion of premiums will be transferred to reinsurance companies to share the risk.</p>

<AIContent content={`<p><strong>Definition:</strong> Ceded premium refers to the premium that an insurance company receives for the insurance risk it undertakes during a specified period as agreed in the insurance contract. For example, if the ratio is 70:30, the insurance company bears 70% of the risk and premium, while the reinsurance company bears 30%. The advantage of this method is its simplicity, but the downside is that the insurance company still bears a significant portion of the risk when the risk is high.</li><li><strong>Non-Proportional Reinsurance:</strong> In this type of reinsurance, the insurance company and the reinsurance company share the risk based on a predetermined loss limit. The advantage of this method is better risk control for the insurance company, but the contracts are more complex.</li></ul></p><p><strong>Specific Cases:</strong><ul><li><strong>Case 1:</strong> Insurance Company A signs a proportional reinsurance contract with Reinsurance Company B with a ratio of 60:40.
