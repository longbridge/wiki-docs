# Distribution Yield

<ArticleMeta id={101394} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 19:45:12'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Distribution Yield is the ratio of the cash distributions paid by an investment fund or trust over a specific period to its current market price. This yield is typically expressed as an annualized percentage and is calculated by dividing the total distributions paid over the past 12 months by the current market price. Distribution Yield reflects the cash return to investors from holding the fund's shares, excluding any capital appreciation.</p>
