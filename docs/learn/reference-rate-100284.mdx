# Reference Rate

<ArticleMeta id={100284} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A Reference Rate, also known as a Benchmark Rate, is a standard interest rate widely used in financial transactions and loan pricing. It is typically published by governments or financial institutions and is used to determine other interest rates, such as those for loans, deposits, and derivative contracts. Common examples of reference rates include the London Interbank Offered Rate (LIBOR), the U.S. Federal Funds Rate, and the European Central Bank's Main Refinancing Rate. Changes in reference rates directly impact financial markets and economic activities, making them closely watched indicators.</p>
