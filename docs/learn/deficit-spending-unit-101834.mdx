# Deficit Spending Unit

<ArticleMeta id={101834} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:58'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

A deficit spending unit is an economic term used to describe how an economy, or an economic group within that economy, has spent more than it has earned over a specified measurement period. Both companies and governments may experience a deficit spending unit.

This deficit may be used for public infrastructure, social welfare, etc.</li><li><strong>Corporate Deficit:</strong> When a company's expenditures exceed its revenues in an accounting period, it typically covers the deficit by borrowing or issuing stock.
