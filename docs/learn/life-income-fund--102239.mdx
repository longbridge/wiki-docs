# Life Income Fund 

<ArticleMeta id={102239} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 17:15:12'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A Life Income Fund (LIF) is a retirement income arrangement designed to provide retirees with regular income. These funds are typically created by transferring retirement savings into the LIF, which then pays out income to the holder based on government-regulated minimum and maximum withdrawal limits. The primary purpose of an LIF is to ensure that retirement savings continue to provide income throughout the retiree's life.</p><p>Key characteristics of a life income fund include:</p><ol><li><strong>Retirement Income</strong>: An LIF converts retirement savings into a regular income stream, ensuring financial stability during retirement.</li><li><strong>Withdrawal Limits</strong>: Governments typically set minimum and maximum withdrawal limits for LIFs to prevent excessive withdrawals that could deplete the funds prematurely.</li><li><strong>Tax Deferral</strong>: Investment earnings within an LIF typically benefit from tax deferral until withdrawals are made, at which point taxes are due.</li><li><strong>Flexibility</strong>: The holder can choose the withdrawal amount within the prescribed limits based on their financial needs.</li></ol><p>LIFs are common in Canada and some other countries as a tool for managing retirement income, helping retirees better plan and manage their retirement savings.
