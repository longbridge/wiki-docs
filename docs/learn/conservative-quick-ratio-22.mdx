# Conservative Quick Ratio

<ArticleMeta id={22} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 21:10:44'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

The conservative quick ratio is the ratio of a company's quick assets to its current liabilities. Quick assets are assets that can be quickly converted into cash, such as cash, short-term investments, and accounts receivable, while current liabilities are debts that a company needs to repay in the short term. The conservative quick ratio is a measure of a company's short-term solvency, and a higher conservative quick ratio indicates a stronger ability to repay debts.
