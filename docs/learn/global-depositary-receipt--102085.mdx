# Global Depositary Receipt 

<ArticleMeta id={102085} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 03:32:49'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A Global Depositary Receipt (GDR) is a financial instrument that allows a company's shares issued in one country to be traded on securities markets in another country. GDRs are issued by a depositary bank and represent a certain number of shares of the company in its home market. Investors who purchase GDRs indirectly own shares in the foreign company and can benefit from dividends and capital appreciation.</p><p>Key characteristics include:</p><ol><li><strong>Cross-Border Trading</strong>: GDRs enable companies to raise capital in international markets, and investors can buy and trade them on different securities exchanges.</li><li><strong>Depositary Bank</strong>: Issued and managed by depositary banks (such as Citibank, JPMorgan Chase), which hold the actual shares and issue corresponding depositary receipts.</li><li><strong>Simplified Investment</strong>: Provides investors with a simplified way to indirectly invest in foreign company shares without the need to open a trading account in the foreign market.</li><li><strong>Liquidity</strong>: Enhances the liquidity of the shares, allowing the company to attract more international investors.</li></ol><p>The issuance process of Global Depositary Receipts:</p><ol><li><strong>Company Authorization</strong>: The company authorizes a depositary bank to issue GDRs.</li><li><strong>Share Deposit</strong>: The company issues shares in its home market and deposits them with the depositary bank.</li><li><strong>Receipt Issuance</strong>: The depositary bank issues a corresponding number of GDRs based on the deposited shares.</li><li><strong>Market Trading</strong>: Investors buy and trade GDRs on international securities markets.</li></ol><p>A global depositary receipt is very similar to an American depositary receipt (ADR) except that an ADR only lists shares of a foreign company in U.S. markets.</p>
