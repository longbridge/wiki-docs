# Loss Carryforward

<ArticleMeta id={100697} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 20:18:58'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p><span style={{backgroundColor:"rgba(0,0,0,0.05)",color:"rgb(36,41,47)"}}>Loss Carryforward, also known as loss carryover, is a tax provision that allows a company to apply a net operating loss (NOL) to future taxable income to reduce tax liability in future years. Depending on the tax regulations of different countries, losses can typically be carried forward for a period ranging from 5 to 20 years, with some jurisdictions allowing for indefinite carryforward. This mechanism helps businesses mitigate tax burdens during tough financial periods, aiding in their recovery and growth.</span></p>
