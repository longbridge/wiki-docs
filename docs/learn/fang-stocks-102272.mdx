# FANG Stocks

<ArticleMeta id={102272} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 17:03:06'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>FANG Stocks refer to four highly influential technology companies in the U.S. stock market: Facebook (now Meta Platforms), Amazon, Netflix, and Google (now Alphabet). The term FANG was first coined by CNBC television host Jim Cramer in 2013 to describe these tech giants' outstanding performance and strong growth potential in the market. FANG stocks are known for their high growth, high returns, and market leadership, attracting significant attention from investors. In recent years, the concept of FANG stocks has expanded to include other tech giants like Apple and Microsoft, forming variations such as FAANG or FAAMG.</p>
