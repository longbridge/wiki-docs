# Facultative Reinsurance

<ArticleMeta id={102173} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:59:00'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Facultative Reinsurance is a type of reinsurance arrangement where the primary insurer (ceding company) negotiates and arranges reinsurance coverage for specific risks with the reinsurer (assuming company) on a case-by-case basis. Each risk is individually underwritten and agreed upon, rather than being automatically covered under a long-term contract. Facultative reinsurance is typically used for particularly complex or large risks that may not be covered under standard reinsurance contracts.</p><p>Key characteristics include:</p><p>Individual Handling: Each risk is negotiated and arranged individually, with the reinsurer underwriting and accepting each risk on a case-by-case basis.<br/>High Flexibility: The primary insurer can choose the most suitable reinsurer and terms based on the specific risk.<br/>Non-Automatic: Unlike automatic reinsurance contracts, facultative reinsurance requires individual handling of each risk, making the process more cumbersome.<br/>Customization: Reinsurance terms can be customized to fit the specific risk, meeting special needs.<br/>Example of Facultative Reinsurance application:<br/>Suppose a primary insurer underwrites a large commercial building with unique design and high value. Due to the building's specific characteristics, the primary insurer seeks reinsurance coverage. The primary insurer negotiates with several reinsurers and finally reaches a facultative reinsurance agreement with one reinsurer to cover part of the risk associated with the building. After detailed assessment, the reinsurer agrees to provide the reinsurance coverage.</p>
