# Mortgage-Backed Securities

<ArticleMeta id={102298} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 01:52:58'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Mortgage-Backed Securities (MBS) refer to the process of bundling a group of mortgage loans into securities and selling them to investors. The value and returns of these securities are derived from the principal and interest payments of the underlying mortgage loans. By securitizing mortgages, lending institutions can convert their loan portfolios into tradable financial instruments, increase liquidity, and distribute risk among numerous investors. MBS can be categorized into various types, including mortgage-backed bonds and mortgage pass-through certificates. Mortgage securitization plays a significant role in financial markets and is widely used in residential and commercial real estate financing. However, MBS can also face credit risk, market risk, and other issues, especially when the default rates of the underlying loans are high.</p>
