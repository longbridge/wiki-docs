# West Texas Intermediate 

<ArticleMeta id={102733} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 14:35:45'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>West Texas Intermediate (WTI) is a grade of crude oil and one of the main three benchmarks in oil pricing, along with Brent and Dubai Crude. WTI is considered a high-quality oil that is relatively easy to refine.</p><p>WTI is known as a light sweet oil. It contains less than 0.50% sulfur, whereas the norm is about 0.24% to 0.34%, making it "sweet." It also has a low density, making it "light."</p><p>WTI is the underlying commodity of the New York Mercantile Exchange's (NYMEX) oil futures contract.</p>
