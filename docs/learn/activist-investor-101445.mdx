# Activist Investor

<ArticleMeta id={101445} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:54'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>An activist investor is an individual or group that purchases significant shares in a company and seeks to influence its management and strategic decisions to enhance the company's value. Activist investors typically engage in various actions to achieve their objectives, including but not limited to:</p><ol><li>Proposing Board Members: Activist investors may nominate new board members to change the company's governance structure.</li><li>Pushing for Restructuring: They may push for the company to undergo restructuring, spin-offs, or the sale of certain assets to increase overall value.</li><li>Public Pressure: By releasing open letters or leveraging media coverage, they exert pressure on the company's management to take specific actions.</li><li>Gaining Shareholder Support: Activist investors often seek the support of other shareholders to amplify their influence.</li></ol><p>The primary goal of activist investors is to improve shareholder value by enhancing the company's operational efficiency, governance, or strategic direction. While their actions can be controversial, they can sometimes lead to positive changes within the company.</p>
