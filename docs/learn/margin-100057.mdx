# Margin

<ArticleMeta id={100057} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>In&nbsp;finance,&nbsp;the margin&nbsp;is the&nbsp;collateral&nbsp;that an investor has to deposit with their&nbsp;broker&nbsp;or exchange to cover the&nbsp;credit risk&nbsp;the holder poses for the broker or the exchange. An investor can create credit risk if they borrow cash from the broker to buy financial instruments, borrow financial instruments to sell them&nbsp;short, or enter into a&nbsp;derivative&nbsp;contract.</p><p>Buying on&nbsp;margin&nbsp;occurs when an investor buys an asset by borrowing the balance from a broker. Buying on margin refers to the initial payment made to the broker for the asset; the investor uses the marginable securities in their brokerage account as&nbsp;collateral.&nbsp;</p><p>In a general business context, the margin is the difference between a product or service's&nbsp;selling price and the cost of production, or the ratio of profit to revenue. Margin&nbsp;can also refer to the portion of the interest rate on an adjustable-rate mortgage&nbsp;(ARM) added to the adjustment-index rate.</p>
