# Long-Term Payables

<ArticleMeta id={328} updatedAt={'2024-09-13 11:55:11'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Long-term payables refer to amounts a company owes that are expected to be paid over a period exceeding one year or one operating cycle, whichever is longer. These payables typically arise from installment purchases, long-term lease expenses, or long-term loans. Long-term payables are presented as non-current liabilities on the balance sheet, and companies need to reasonably estimate their value and make necessary financial adjustments.</p>
