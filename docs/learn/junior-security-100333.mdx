# Junior Security

<ArticleMeta id={100333} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A Junior Security refers to a type of security that holds a lower priority in the capital structure of a company. In the event of liquidation or bankruptcy, junior securities are repaid after senior securities. These typically include subordinated bonds and junior equity. Due to their lower priority, junior securities carry higher risk, but they also potentially offer higher returns. Investors considering junior securities need to be aware of the higher credit risk and liquidity risk associated with these instruments.</p>
