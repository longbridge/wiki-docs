# Risk Allocation

<ArticleMeta id={100437} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Risk allocation refers to the strategy of distributing investment funds across different asset classes to reduce overall investment risk. This approach aims to mitigate the impact of volatility in any single asset on the overall portfolio by diversifying investments among various assets such as stocks, bonds, real estate, and cash. The core idea of risk allocation is that different asset classes have low correlations with each other, so when one asset performs poorly, others may perform well, thereby smoothing overall returns. Effective risk allocation helps investors manage and control risk while pursuing returns.</p>
