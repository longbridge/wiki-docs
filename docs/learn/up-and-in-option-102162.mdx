# Up-And-In Option

<ArticleMeta id={102162} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 17:40:20'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>An Up-and-In Option is a type of barrier option that becomes activated and turns into a regular option only if the price of the underlying asset reaches or exceeds a predetermined barrier level (the knock-in price). Up-and-In Options can be either call options or put options and are primarily used for hedging or speculating on specific market movements.</p><p>Key characteristics include:</p><p>Barrier Level: The option becomes effective only if the underlying asset's price reaches or exceeds the predefined barrier level.<br/>Option Type: Can be either a call option or a put option, depending on the investor's market expectations.<br/>Lower Premium: Due to the activation condition, the premium for an Up-and-In Option is typically lower than that of a regular option.<br/>Risk and Reward: Offers protection or profit opportunities under specific conditions, but if the barrier is not reached, the option expires worthless, and the investor loses the premium paid.<br/>Example of Up-and-In Option application:<br/>Suppose an investor buys an Up-and-In Call Option on an underlying asset currently priced at $100, with a barrier level of $120 and a strike price of $125. If the underlying asset's price reaches or exceeds $120 during the option's life, the option is activated and becomes a regular call option, giving the investor the right to buy the asset at $125 upon expiry. If the underlying asset's price never reaches $120, the option expires worthless, and the investor loses the premium paid for the option.</p>
