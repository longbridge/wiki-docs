# Financial Distress

<ArticleMeta id={100280} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Financial Distress refers to a situation where an individual or organization is unable to meet its financial obligations, such as paying off debts, interest, or other financial commitments on time. This condition can be caused by various factors, including declining revenues, rising costs, adverse market conditions, or poor management. When a company experiences financial distress, it may face the risk of bankruptcy, restructuring, or liquidation. Financial distress not only affects the operation and value of the company but can also have cascading effects on its suppliers, customers, and the broader economy. Companies in financial distress typically need to take urgent measures, such as cutting costs, restructuring debt, raising new capital, or seeking bankruptcy protection, to navigate through the crisis.</p>
