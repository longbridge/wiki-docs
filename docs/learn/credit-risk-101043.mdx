# Credit Risk

<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Credit risk is the probability of a financial loss resulting from a borrower's failure to repay a loan.&nbsp;</p>

<AIContent content={`<p><strong>Definition:</strong> Credit risk refers to the probability of financial loss resulting from a borrower's failure to repay a loan. It is one of the primary risks faced by financial institutions when providing loans or credit. Credit risk is also known as default risk.</p><p><strong>Origin:</strong> The concept of credit risk dates back to ancient times when lending activities already existed. With the development of financial markets, especially in the 19th and 20th centuries, credit risk management gradually became a crucial topic for banks and financial institutions. The introduction of the Basel Accords in the 1980s marked the beginning of modern credit risk management.</p><p><strong>Categories and Characteristics:</strong> Credit risk can be divided into the following categories:<ul><li><strong>Individual Credit Risk:</strong> Refers to the risk of default by a single borrower, usually measured through credit scoring and financial status assessment.</li><li><strong>Portfolio Credit Risk:</strong> Refers to the risk of default within an entire loan portfolio, typically managed through diversification and risk hedging.</li><li><strong>Systemic Credit Risk:</strong> Refers to large-scale default risk caused by macroeconomic factors such as economic recessions or financial crises.</li></ul>Characteristics of credit risk include:<ul><li>Unpredictability: Borrower default behavior is difficult to fully predict.</li><li>High Impact: Defaults can lead to significant financial losses.</li><li>Manageability: Credit risk can be partially managed and controlled through credit assessment, collateral, and insurance.</li></ul></p><p><strong>Specific Cases:</strong><ul><li><strong>Case 1:</strong> A bank provides a loan to a small and medium-sized enterprise, but due to poor business performance, the enterprise fails to repay the loan on time, resulting in financial losses for the bank. This is a typical example of individual credit risk.</li><li><strong>Case 2:</strong> During the 2008 financial crisis, a large number of borrowers were unable to repay their mortgage loans, leading to significant losses for banks and financial institutions. This is an example of systemic credit risk.</li></ul></p><p><strong>Common Questions:</strong><ul><li><strong>How to assess credit risk?</strong> It is usually assessed through credit scoring, financial statement analysis, and borrower history.</li><li><strong>How to manage credit risk?</strong> It can be managed through diversification, setting loan limits, requiring collateral, and purchasing credit insurance.</li><li><strong>What is the difference between credit risk and market risk?</strong> Credit risk mainly involves borrower default, while market risk involves market price fluctuations.</li></ul></p>`} id={101043} />
