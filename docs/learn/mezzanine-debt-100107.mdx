# Mezzanine Debt

<ArticleMeta id={100107} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Mezzanine debt occurs when a hybrid debt issue is subordinated to another debt issue from the same issuer. Mezzanine debt has embedded equity instruments attached, often known as warrants, which increase the attractiveness of the subordinated debt and allow greater flexibility when dealing with bondholders.</p><p>Mezzanine debt is frequently associated with acquisitions and buyouts, for which it may be used to rank new owners higher than existing owners in the stakeholder seniority list in case of company liquidation.</p>
