# Boundary Conditions

<ArticleMeta id={100264} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Boundary conditions refer to the additional constraints required to solve differential equations or other equations in fields such as mathematics, physics, and engineering. These conditions define specific values or behaviors that the solution function and its derivatives must satisfy at the boundaries of the problem. Boundary conditions can be fixed values (Dirichlet conditions), derivative values (Neumann conditions), or a linear combination of both (Robin conditions). In finance, boundary conditions are often used to determine the solution to financial models, such as the boundary conditions in option pricing models, ensuring the uniqueness and stability of the solution.</p>
