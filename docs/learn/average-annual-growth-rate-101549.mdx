# Average Annual Growth Rate

<ArticleMeta id={101549} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 19:37:00'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

The average annual growth rate (AAGR) reports the mean increase in the value of an individual investment, portfolio, asset, or cash flow on an annualized basis. It doesn't take compounding into account.

Suitable for situations where growth rates do not vary significantly.</li><li><strong>Weighted Average Method:</strong> Calculates the average growth rate based on the weight of each year, suitable for situations with significant fluctuations in growth rates.</li></ul>The main characteristic of AAGR is its simplicity and ease of understanding, but it does not consider the effect of compounding, which may underestimate the actual growth of long-term investments.</p><p><strong>Specific Cases:</strong><ul><li><strong>Case 1:</strong> Suppose an investment has growth rates of 5%, 10%, and 15% over three years.
