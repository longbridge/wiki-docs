# Chattel Mortgage

<ArticleMeta id={100387} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A Chattel Mortgage is a loan agreement where the borrower uses movable personal property (chattel) such as vehicles, equipment, or inventory as collateral to secure the loan. Unlike a real estate mortgage, which involves immovable property like land or buildings, a chattel mortgage involves movable assets.</p><p>In a chattel mortgage agreement, the borrower receives the loan and transfers the ownership of the chattel to the lender (usually a bank or financial institution), but the borrower retains the right to use the chattel. Once the borrower repays the loan as agreed, the ownership of the chattel is transferred back to the borrower. If the borrower defaults on the loan, the lender has the right to seize and sell the chattel to recover the loan amount.</p>
