# Risk Premium

<ArticleMeta id={100438} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Risk premium is the additional return that investors require for holding a risky asset instead of a risk-free asset. It reflects investors' aversion to risk and the market's pricing of risk. Generally, the higher the risk of an asset, the higher its risk premium. For instance, the expected return on stocks is usually higher than that on government bonds because stocks are more volatile and uncertain. The risk premium can be calculated by comparing the expected returns of different assets; for example, the risk premium for stocks is the expected return on stocks minus the risk-free rate (such as the government bond rate).</p>
