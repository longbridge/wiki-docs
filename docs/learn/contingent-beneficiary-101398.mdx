# Contingent Beneficiary

<ArticleMeta id={101398} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:54'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A Contingent Beneficiary, also known as a Secondary Beneficiary, is an individual or entity designated to receive inheritance or insurance benefits if the primary beneficiary is unable to do so. For instance, if the primary beneficiary passes away, is missing, or cannot legally accept the assets, the contingent beneficiary steps in to receive them. Naming a contingent beneficiary ensures that the assets are smoothly transferred and avoids potential legal disputes or delays that may arise if the primary beneficiary cannot fulfill their role.</p>
