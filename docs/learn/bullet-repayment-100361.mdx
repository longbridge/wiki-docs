# Bullet Repayment

<ArticleMeta id={100361} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Bullet repayment refers to a repayment method where the borrower repays the entire principal amount in one lump sum at the end of the loan term. During the loan period, the borrower typically only pays interest, and the principal is due in full upon maturity. This repayment method is commonly used for short-term loans or structured financial products such as bonds or certain types of mortgages. The advantage of bullet repayment is that it reduces the borrower's cash flow burden during the loan period, but it requires the borrower to have sufficient funds to repay the principal at maturity.</p>
