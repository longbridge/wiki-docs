# Multi-Asset Class

<ArticleMeta id={100406} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Multi-Asset Class refers to an investment strategy that involves holding a diversified portfolio containing various types of asset classes. These asset classes can include equities, bonds, cash, real estate, commodities, hedge funds, private equity, and others. The primary objective of multi-asset class investing is to reduce risk and enhance potential returns through diversification.</p><p>Common multi-asset class investment strategies include:</p><ul><li>Strategic Asset Allocation: Determining the target weights for each asset class based on long-term investment goals and risk tolerance, and periodically rebalancing the portfolio.</li><li>Tactical Asset Allocation: Adjusting the weights of different asset classes based on short-term market conditions and opportunities to achieve excess returns.</li><li>Dynamic Asset Allocation: Combining strategic and tactical asset allocation approaches to dynamically adjust the portfolio based on market changes.</li></ul>
