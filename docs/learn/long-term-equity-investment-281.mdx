# Long-Term Equity Investment

<ArticleMeta id={281} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 20:57:28'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Long-term equity investment refers to the equity investments made by a company in other enterprises with the intention of obtaining long-term returns, typically held for more than one year. These investments include equity stakes in subsidiaries, associates, or joint ventures. Long-term equity investments can be accounted for using the equity method or the cost method, depending on the degree of control the investing company has over the investee. This type of investment reflects the company's equity stake in other entities and its long-term strategic positioning.</p>
