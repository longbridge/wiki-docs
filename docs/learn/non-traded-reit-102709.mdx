# Non-Traded REIT

<ArticleMeta id={102709} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:59:02'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Non-traded REITs are not listed on public exchanges and can provide retail investors access to inaccessible real estate investments with tax benefits.</p>

They generate income through rental income and property appreciation.</li><li><strong>Mortgage REITs:</strong> These invest primarily in real estate mortgages and other real estate debt instruments, earning income through interest payments.</li></ul>Characteristics of Non-Traded REITs include:<ul><li>Low liquidity: Since they are not traded on public markets, investors typically need to hold them for a longer period to realize returns.</li><li>Potential for high returns: Due to their investments in the real estate market, Non-Traded REITs can potentially offer higher yields.</li><li>Tax benefits: Qualifying Non-Traded REITs can enjoy certain tax advantages.</li></ul></p><p><strong>Specific Cases:</strong><ul><li><strong>Case 1:</strong> An investor purchases a Non-Traded Equity REIT that primarily invests in commercial real estate in major cities.
