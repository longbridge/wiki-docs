# Loan Note

<ArticleMeta id={100352} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A loan note is a formal legal document that outlines the terms and conditions of a loan agreement between a borrower and a lender. The loan note typically includes details such as the loan amount, interest rate, repayment schedule, repayment method, and any default terms. It serves as a debt instrument, evidencing the borrower's promise to repay the loan under the agreed-upon conditions. Loan notes can be either secured (e.g., a mortgage) or unsecured (e.g., a personal loan). Once the loan note is signed, both the borrower and the lender are legally bound by its terms, and the borrower must make timely repayments to avoid potential legal consequences.</p>
