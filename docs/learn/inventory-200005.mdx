# Inventory

<ArticleMeta id={200005} updatedAt={'2024-09-05 21:24:50'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Inventory refers to the goods a company holds for the purpose of resale, work-in-progress items, and materials used in the production process.</p>

<AIContent content={`<p><strong>Definition:</strong> Inventory refers to the goods held by a business for sale in the ordinary course of business, work-in-progress, and materials consumed in the production process. Inventory is a crucial part of a company's current assets and typically includes raw materials, work-in-progress, and finished goods.</p><p><strong>Origin:</strong> The concept of inventory management dates back to early commercial activities when merchants needed to manage and record their goods to ensure a smooth supply chain. With the development of the Industrial Revolution, inventory management became more systematic and complex, especially in the context of mass production and global trade.</p><p><strong>Categories and Characteristics:</strong> Inventory can be classified into the following categories:<ul><li><strong>Raw Materials:</strong> Basic materials used in the production process, such as steel, wood, etc.</li><li><strong>Work-in-Progress:</strong> Products that are in the process of being manufactured but are not yet complete.</li><li><strong>Finished Goods:</strong> Products that are completed and ready for sale.</li><li><strong>Maintenance, Repair, and Operations (MRO) Supplies:</strong> Parts used for maintenance and repairs.</li></ul>Each type of inventory has its unique characteristics and requirements in terms of management and accounting.</p><p><strong>Specific Cases:</strong><ol><li><strong>Case 1:</strong> An automobile manufacturing company holds a large amount of steel (raw materials), which is processed into car parts (work-in-progress) and finally assembled into cars (finished goods) for sale.</li><li><strong>Case 2:</strong> An electronics retailer holds various models of smartphones (finished goods) and also maintains spare parts for repairs (MRO supplies).</li></ol></p><p><strong>Common Questions:</strong><ul><li><strong>How to determine the value of inventory?</strong> The value of inventory is usually determined by the cost method or market value method.</li><li><strong>What are common pitfalls in inventory management?</strong> Common pitfalls include excessive inventory leading to high capital tie-up and insufficient inventory causing supply chain disruptions.</li></ul></p>`} id={200005} />
