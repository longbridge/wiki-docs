# EPS Guidance

<ArticleMeta id={104564} updatedAt={'2023-10-25 02:20:11'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

Earnings per share forecast (EPS guidance) refers to the forecast of expected earnings per share for a certain period of time in the future. This forecast can be used by investors to assess the company's future profitability and growth prospects.

<AIContent content={`<p><strong>Definition:</strong> Earnings Per Share (EPS) guidance refers to a company's forecast of its expected earnings per share for a future period. This forecast helps investors assess the company's future profitability and growth prospects. Initially, EPS guidance appeared in annual reports, but over time, it also began to be included in quarterly reports and special announcements.</p><p><strong>Categories and Characteristics:</strong> EPS guidance can be divided into two categories: quantitative guidance and qualitative guidance.<ul><li><strong>Quantitative Guidance:</strong> The company provides specific numerical forecasts for EPS, usually expressed as a range, such as EPS will be between $1.00 and $1.20. This type of guidance is straightforward and allows investors to perform quantitative analysis.</li><li><strong>Qualitative Guidance:</strong> The company provides descriptive information about future EPS without specific numbers. For example, the company might state that it expects future EPS to grow or remain stable. This type of guidance is more vague but can be more appropriate in certain situations.</li></ul></p><p><strong>Specific Cases:</strong><ul><li><strong>Case 1:</strong> Apple Inc. provides EPS guidance in its quarterly earnings report, forecasting that EPS will be between $3.00 and $3.20 for the next quarter.
