# Home Equity

<ArticleMeta id={100422} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Home equity refers to the actual value of a homeowner's interest in their property after subtracting any outstanding mortgage or other property-related debts. The calculation is as follows: Home&nbsp;Equity = Current&nbsp;Market&nbsp;Value&nbsp;of&nbsp;the&nbsp;Home − Outstanding&nbsp;Loan&nbsp;Balance.&nbsp;<br/>Home equity represents the portion of the property that the homeowner truly owns. As the home's value increases or the loan balance decreases, home equity also rises. Home equity can be used to apply for a home equity loan or a home equity line of credit (HELOC).</p>
