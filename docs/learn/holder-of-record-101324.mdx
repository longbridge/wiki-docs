# Holder Of Record

<ArticleMeta id={101324} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:54'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

A holder of record is the name of the person who is the registered owner of a security and who has the rights, benefits, and responsibilities of ownership.

<AIContent content={`<h2>Holder Registration</h2><h3>Definition</h3><p>Holder registration refers to the name of the person who is the registered owner of a security and has ownership, benefits, and responsibilities. In simple terms, holder registration records who is the legal holder of a particular security and ensures that the holder enjoys the corresponding rights and assumes the corresponding responsibilities.</p><h3>Origin</h3><p>The concept of holder registration originated in the early development of the securities market. To ensure the legality and transparency of securities transactions, securities issuing institutions began to record the holder information of each security. As the securities market developed, the holder registration system gradually improved and became an important part of the modern securities market.</p><h3>Categories and Characteristics</h3><p>Holder registration is mainly divided into two categories: nominal holder registration and actual holder registration. Nominal holder registration refers to the registration of securities under the name of a nominal holder, but the actual benefits and responsibilities are borne by the actual holder. Actual holder registration refers to the registration of securities under the name of the actual holder, who enjoys ownership, benefits, and responsibilities.</p><p>The characteristics of nominal holder registration are that it is convenient for management and trading, but there may be risks of information opacity; the characteristics of actual holder registration are that the information is transparent, but management and trading are relatively complex.</p><h3>Specific Cases</h3><p>Case 1: A company issues stocks, and investor A purchases 100 shares and registers as the holder. At this time, A's name is recorded in the company's shareholder register, and A enjoys all shareholder rights, such as dividends and voting rights.</p><p>Case 2: Investor B purchases a fund through a securities company, and the fund's holder registration is under the name of the securities company, but the actual benefits and responsibilities are borne by B. In this case, the securities company is the nominal holder, and B is the actual holder.</p><h3>Common Questions</h3><p>1. Can holder registration be changed?<br/>Answer: Yes. The holder can apply to change the holder registration information through the stock exchange or securities company.</p><p>2. Is holder registration information public?<br/>Answer: Generally, holder registration information is confidential, but in some cases (such as legal requirements), it may need to be disclosed.</p>`} id={101324} />
