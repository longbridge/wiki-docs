# Deferred Tax Liability

<ArticleMeta id={100376} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A Deferred Tax Liability (DTL) arises when there is a difference between the accounting income and taxable income, leading to taxes that are owed in the future. This liability is often recorded on a company's balance sheet to reflect the amount of taxes that will be payable in the future due to temporary differences between the book value of assets and liabilities and their tax bases. Common causes of DTL include differences in depreciation methods, timing of revenue and expense recognition, and other accounting treatments. These differences are temporary and will eventually reverse, leading to the settlement of the tax liability.</p>
