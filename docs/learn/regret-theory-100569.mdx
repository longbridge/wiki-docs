# Regret Theory

<ArticleMeta id={100569} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:51'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Regret Theory is a concept in behavioral finance that describes how investors anticipate the emotional impact of regret when making investment decisions. According to Regret Theory, individuals consider not only the potential gains and risks but also the regret they might feel if their choices turn out poorly. To avoid future regret, investors might make decisions that seem irrational, such as being overly conservative or excessively risky. Regret Theory helps explain why investors sometimes deviate from the rational decision-making models of traditional finance, opting instead for strategies that minimize potential future regret.</p>
