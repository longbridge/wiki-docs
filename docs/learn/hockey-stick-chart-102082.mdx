# Hockey Stick Chart

<ArticleMeta id={102082} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 18:33:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A Hockey Stick Chart is a graphical representation used to display data growth patterns characterized by a long period of stable or slow growth followed by a sudden sharp increase, creating a shape resembling a hockey stick. This chart is often used to illustrate company revenue, sales, user growth, or climate change data.</p><p>Key characteristics include:</p><ol><li><strong>Stable Period</strong>: Data remains stable or grows slowly over a long period, forming the "shaft" of the hockey stick.</li><li><strong>Sharp Increase</strong>: At a certain point, data suddenly increases sharply, forming the "blade" of the hockey stick.</li><li><strong>Visual Clarity</strong>: The chart is visually clear and easy to recognize, highlighting growth trends and inflection points.</li><li><strong>Wide Application</strong>: Widely used in business, economics, technology, and climate science to demonstrate and analyze data growth patterns.</li></ol><p>Example applications of a Hockey Stick Chart:</p><ol><li><strong>Company Revenue Growth</strong>: A technology company experiences stable revenue growth during its startup phase, followed by rapid growth after launching a new product, forming a hockey stick chart.</li><li><strong>User Growth</strong>: A social media platform sees slow user growth initially, but after increased marketing efforts, the user base grows rapidly, forming a hockey stick chart.</li><li><strong>Climate Change</strong>: Climate scientists use hockey stick charts to show global temperature changes, illustrating the sharp increase in temperatures since the industrial revolution.</li></ol>
