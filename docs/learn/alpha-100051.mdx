# Alpha

<ArticleMeta id={100051} updatedAt={'2024-09-13 11:52:39'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Alpha (α) is a term used in investing to describe an investment strategy's ability to beat the market, or its "edge."&nbsp;Alpha is thus also often referred to as “excess return” or the “abnormal rate of return” in relation to a benchmark, when adjusted for risk.&nbsp;</p><p>Alpha is often used in conjunction with&nbsp;beta (the Greek letter β), which measures the broad market's overall volatility&nbsp;or&nbsp;risk, known as systematic market risk.</p><p>Alpha is used in finance as a measure of&nbsp;performance, indicating when a strategy, trader, or portfolio manager has managed to beat the market return or other benchmark over some period. Alpha, often considered the&nbsp;active return&nbsp;on an investment, gauges the performance of an investment against a&nbsp;market index&nbsp;or benchmark that is considered to represent the&nbsp;market’s&nbsp;movement as a whole.</p><p>The&nbsp;excess return&nbsp;of an investment relative to the&nbsp;return&nbsp;of a benchmark&nbsp;index&nbsp;is the investment’s alpha. Alpha may be positive or negative and is the result of active investing. Beta, on the other hand, can be earned through passive&nbsp;index&nbsp;investing.</p>
