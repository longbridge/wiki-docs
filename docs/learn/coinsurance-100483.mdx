# Co-Insurance

<ArticleMeta id={100483} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Co-Insurance refers to an arrangement where two or more insurance companies share the risk and responsibility under a single insurance contract. Each insurance company takes on a portion of the premiums and claims according to their agreed share in the contract. This arrangement is typically used for large or high-risk insurance projects to spread the risk and enhance underwriting capacity.</p><p>In the context of U.S. health insurance, co-insurance refers to the arrangement where the insured individual shares the cost of covered healthcare services with the insurance company after meeting the deductible.&nbsp;</p>
