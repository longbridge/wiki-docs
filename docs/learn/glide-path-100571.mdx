# Glide Path

<ArticleMeta id={100571} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:51'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p><span style={{backgroundColor:"rgba(0,0,0,0.05)",color:"rgb(36,41,47)"}}>The Glide Path refers to the gradual shift in asset allocation within a target date fund as the target date approaches. This shift involves moving from higher-risk, higher-return assets like stocks to lower-risk, lower-return assets like bonds. The design of the glide path aims to reduce the investment portfolio's risk as the investor nears retirement, thereby protecting the accumulated wealth. Typically, the glide path maintains a higher proportion of stocks in the early stages to achieve higher long-term returns, while gradually increasing the proportion of bonds and other low-risk assets as the target date nears, thereby minimizing the impact of market volatility on the portfolio.</span></p>
