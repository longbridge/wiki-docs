# Internal Growth Rate

<ArticleMeta id={101540} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:58'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

An internal growth rate (IGR) is the highest level of growth achievable for a business without obtaining outside financing, and a firm's maximum internal growth rate is the level of business operations that can continue to fund and grow the company.

The IGR gradually became an important indicator of a company's endogenous growth capability.</p><p><strong>Categories and Characteristics:</strong> The Internal Growth Rate can be categorized into the following types:<ul><li><strong>Actual Internal Growth Rate:</strong> The actual internal growth level achieved by a company over a specific period, usually calculated from financial statement data.</li><li><strong>Potential Internal Growth Rate:</strong> The maximum growth level a company can achieve under ideal conditions by optimizing internal resource allocation and improving operational efficiency.</li></ul>Characteristics of the Internal Growth Rate include:<ul><li><strong>Self-accumulation:</strong> Relies on the company's own profits and reinvestment of internal resources, without external financing.</li><li><strong>Sustainability:</strong> Reflects the company's ability to sustain long-term growth.</li><li><strong>Lower Risk:</strong> Since it does not rely on external financing, the company's financial risk is relatively lower.</li></ul></p><p><strong>Specific Cases:</strong><ul><li><strong>Case 1:</strong> A manufacturing company improved its internal growth rate by increasing production efficiency and reducing costs.
