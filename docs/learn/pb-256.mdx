# PB

<p>The Price-to-Book Ratio refers to the ratio between a company's book value and market value. The book value refers to the net value of a company's assets minus its debts, while the market value refers to the market value of a company in the stock market. The book-to-market ratio is used to measure the valuation level of a company. If the book-to-market ratio is relatively low, it may indicate that the market valuation of the company is low, and vice versa.</p>
