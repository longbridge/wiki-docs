# Hikkake Pattern

<ArticleMeta id={100380} updatedAt={'2024-09-12 20:36:31'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>The Hikkake Pattern is a price pattern in technical analysis used to identify signals of market reversals or trend continuations. The Hikkake Pattern typically appears on price charts as a short-term price trap, misleading traders into believing that the market will move in one direction, only for the price to quickly reverse and move in the opposite direction.</p><p>The basic structure of the Hikkake Pattern includes:</p><ol><li>An Inside Bar, where the high and low of the second candle are within the range of the first candle.</li><li>A few subsequent candles that break out above or below the Inside Bar, creating a false breakout.</li><li>The price then reverses and breaks out in the opposite direction of the initial breakout, confirming the Hikkake Pattern.</li></ol>
