# Net Cash Flow from Operating Activities

<p>Net cash flow from operating activities refers to the difference between cash inflows and cash outflows generated by a company's operating activities. This indicator reflects the cash income and expenditure of a company's operating activities and can be used to evaluate the company's operational capabilities and cash flow level.</p><p>Net&nbsp;Cash&nbsp;Flow&nbsp;from&nbsp;Operating&nbsp;Activities = Cash&nbsp;Inflows&nbsp;from&nbsp;Operating&nbsp;Activities − Cash&nbsp;Outflows&nbsp;from&nbsp;Operating&nbsp;Activities</p>
