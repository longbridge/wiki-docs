# Add-On Interest

<ArticleMeta id={100450} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Add-On Interest is a method of calculating interest on a loan where the interest is computed at the beginning of the loan and then spread evenly over the entire term of the loan. Unlike the traditional amortized method, where interest is calculated on the declining balance of the loan, add-on interest typically results in higher total interest payments because it is based on the initial principal amount for the entire loan period. The method of calculating add-on interest is usually specified in the loan agreement and is commonly used in certain types of loans, such as auto loans or installment plans.</p>
