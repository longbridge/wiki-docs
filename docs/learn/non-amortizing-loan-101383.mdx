# Non-Amortizing Loan

<ArticleMeta id={101383} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:54'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A non-amortizing loan is a type of loan where the borrower is required to pay only the interest during the term of the loan, with the principal being repaid in a lump sum at the end of the loan term. Because only interest is paid during the loan period, the monthly payments are typically lower compared to amortizing loans. However, the borrower faces a significant financial obligation at the end of the term when the principal must be repaid in full. Non-amortizing loans are commonly found in certain commercial loans, bonds, and some types of real estate loans.</p>
