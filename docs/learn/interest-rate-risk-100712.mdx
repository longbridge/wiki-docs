# Interest Rate Risk

<ArticleMeta id={100712} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:51'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p><span style={{backgroundColor:"rgba(0,0,0,0.05)",color:"rgb(36,41,47)"}}>Interest Rate Risk refers to the risk that changes in market interest rates will cause fluctuations in the value of financial assets or liabilities. This risk primarily affects bonds, loans, and other fixed-income securities. For investors holding fixed-income assets, an increase in interest rates will lead to a decrease in the market value of these assets, while a decrease in interest rates will increase their market value. Banks, insurance companies, and other financial institutions typically manage and hedge interest rate risk through interest rate swaps, options, and other derivatives. Effective management of interest rate risk is crucial for maintaining the stability and profitability of financial institutions.</span></p>
