# Negative Gearing

<ArticleMeta id={100443} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Negative gearing is an investment strategy commonly used in real estate and other types of investments. In this strategy, the costs of owning and managing an investment property (such as loan interest, property management fees, and maintenance costs) exceed the income generated by the property (such as rental income), resulting in a net loss. This net loss can be used to offset other taxable income, thereby reducing the overall tax liability. The primary goal of negative gearing is to take advantage of tax benefits to reduce current tax burdens while expecting that the property's long-term capital appreciation will eventually outweigh the initial losses.</p>
