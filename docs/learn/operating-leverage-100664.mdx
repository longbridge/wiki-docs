# Operating Leverage

<ArticleMeta id={100664} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:51'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Operating leverage refers to the proportion of fixed costs in a company's total costs and how this proportion affects the company's profitability. High operating leverage means that a company has higher fixed costs relative to its variable costs. In this scenario, changes in sales revenue have a significant impact on the company's profits because fixed costs remain constant regardless of sales volume. Companies with high operating leverage can achieve higher profits when sales increase, but they also face greater risk of losses when sales decline. The degree of operating leverage can be measured by the ratio of fixed costs to variable costs. Understanding operating leverage is crucial for managers in making financial and operational decisions.</p><p><br/>&nbsp;</p>
