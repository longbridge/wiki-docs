# Return On Risk-Adjusted Capital 

<ArticleMeta id={102710} updatedAt={'2024-09-11 23:43:29'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>The return on risk-adjusted capital (RORAC) is a rate of return measure commonly used in financial analysis, where various projects, endeavors, and investments are evaluated based on capital at risk. Projects with different risk profiles are easier to compare with each other once their individual RORAC values have been calculated.</p><p>The RORAC is similar to return on equity (ROE), except the denominator is adjusted to account for the risk of a project.</p>
