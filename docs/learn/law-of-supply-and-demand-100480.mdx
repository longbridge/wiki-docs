# Law Of Supply And Demand

<ArticleMeta id={100480} updatedAt={'2023-09-26 13:58:47'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>The Law of Supply and Demand is a fundamental economic principle that describes the relationship between the availability of a product (supply) and the desire for that product (demand). Specifically:</p><ul><li>Law of Supply: All else being equal, an increase in the price of a good will result in an increase in the quantity supplied. Conversely, a decrease in the price will result in a decrease in the quantity supplied. This occurs because higher prices can lead to higher profits, attracting more producers to the market.</li><li>Law of Demand: All else being equal, an increase in the price of a good will result in a decrease in the quantity demanded. Conversely, a decrease in the price will result in an increase in the quantity demanded. This happens because higher prices reduce consumers' purchasing power or willingness to buy.</li></ul>
