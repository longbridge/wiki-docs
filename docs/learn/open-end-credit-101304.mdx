# Open-End Credit

<ArticleMeta id={101304} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:58:54'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>Open-end credit, also known as revolving credit, is a flexible borrowing arrangement that allows borrowers to draw funds and repay them within a specified credit limit at any time. Common forms of open-end credit include credit cards and revolving credit accounts. Borrowers can access funds multiple times up to their credit limit without needing to apply for a new loan each time. They are required to make at least the minimum payment or pay off the balance in full each billing cycle, along with any applicable interest. The key feature of open-end credit is its flexibility and convenience, but it can also lead to debt accumulation if not managed carefully. Therefore, it is important for borrowers to manage their finances prudently to avoid excessive debt.</p>
