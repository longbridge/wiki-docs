# Fully Amortizing Payment

<ArticleMeta id={102083} updatedAt={'2023-09-25 22:59:00'} alias={`[]`} />
<div className='border-solid border-b border-t-0 my-4 border-[var(--ifm-color-gray-300)]' />

<p>A Fully Amortizing Payment refers to a repayment method where a loan is fully paid off over its term through a series of regular, equal payments. Each payment includes portions that cover both interest and principal. Over time, the interest portion decreases, and the principal portion increases, ensuring the loan is completely paid off by the end of the term.</p><p>Key characteristics include:</p><ol><li><strong>Regular Payments</strong>: Equal payments made at regular intervals, typically monthly.</li><li><strong>Interest and Principal</strong>: Each payment consists of both interest and principal portions, with the interest portion decreasing and the principal portion increasing over time.</li><li><strong>Full Repayment</strong>: By the end of the loan term, both the principal and interest are fully repaid.</li></ol>
